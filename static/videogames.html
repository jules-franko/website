My other favorite videogame other than Minecraft is Silent Hill 3. My first Silent Hill game was the second one, and at first that was my favorite, but as time went on I came to like the third game better. It has an atmosphere unlike any other videogame I have played, the art and aesthetic appeal is perfect, it's subtle, makes itself interesting, and it's also genuinely pretty scary. Overall it just feels high quality and well crafted. Silent Hill 3 has attached itself to me and I have continued to grow an appreciation for it since. I'm not really a hardcore gamer and I don't enjoy that many videogames but something about this one is just how I feel videogames should present themselves.


Heather Mason, the protagonist of this game, is my favorite Silent Hill character. All of the characters from the first four games are good but she feels more human-like than the rest. James was admittedly more pyschologically interesting but Heather adds so much personality to the whole thing. Don't you think blondes have more fun?


You probably don't really care to hear my personal review of the game though (even though you just did). However, if you are interested in it, I would recommend checking out a youtube channel called Lying Figure. His channel description is just "I love Silent Hill" (me too brother). Watch his "lore-plays" where he will play through the entire game, show you every area, every hidden nod, explain all of the story, references, facts about development, etc.

Minecraft is in my opinion, by far the best video game ever created. I'm not sure if it's objectively better than other videogames, but it's certainly my personal favorite. There is no videogame more shrouded in nostalgia and good memories than Minecraft.


My favorite versions of minecraft are anything from the beta to java 1.12. Starting with 1.13, the aquatic update, I started to like the new updates less but they were still okay. What I miss the most is that the older versions of the game had very strange and unique terrain generation that led to very memorable worlds. Everything was better when I was there and worse when I wasn'tTM.


I'm not a fan of the bedrock version of the game. It has microtransactions, horrible optimization, emotes, and different redstone mechanics than the PC version. I sincerely hope Microsoft does not do away with the Java edition to replace it with bedrock. May Notch save us.
